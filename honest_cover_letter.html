Honest Cover Letter

Hey there,

This opportunity looks too good to be true. Instead of teaching myself how to program for hours each evening I can get paid to learn?

You have got to hire me. First off, I am a genius. Don’t believe me? Well all I have to do is pay my annual dues and I will be a member of MENSA again. Not to mention four days a week I find a way to make statistics interesting to a bunch of late teens and early twenty-somethings who really don’t want to be there.

So yes, I’m old. But isn’t everybody up there in D.C. always talking about how we need to retrain mid-career workers to code? Well here I am. Plus I am past the party stage so I won’t be coming into work hungover and I can actually concentrate on the task at hand.

Furthermore, I’m really cool. I have a dog and an SUV (Ford Escape, 2012). I play the guitar (pretty well but I’m out of practice) and I speak Spanish (really well and I practice a lot). And all the women seem to love my red hair.

And I really do work hard. I have been accepted to a Ph.D. in finance starting this fall and I also own a business selling fashion accessories in South America, both of which give me ulcers to think about. But I'm still just lazy enough to be a good programmer.

Anyway, let me know if I might be a fit. If not I’ll just keep on doing what I’m doing. But just remember, when I make it big I will definitely remember my friends!

So, in summary:

  • MENSA
  • Dog
  • SUV
  • Guitar
  • Spanish
  • Friends
  • Did this myself in less than an hour

Later,

Ben
